 March 18, 1944

Tribune: "10,000 Foe Troops Encircled in Burma.... Nippon forces start annihilative operations.... Enemy units in Kohima sector doomed."

We now have 164 consumer cooperatives organized along N.A. lines to do what the government and Japanese can't do — bring rice into Manila.

What Paulino Gullas actually told Laurel: Guerrillas in the south are quiet — but in contact with Americans and receiving supplies periodically.

News: American Liberators bombed Surabaya and Bali; British troops landed behind Japanese positions in the Arakan front, and airborne troops landed somewhere in the Hukawng area behind Japanese positions; the Russians are 63 miles into Poland.
